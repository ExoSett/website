ExoSett stories

What becomes possible when buildings can change?

ExoSett stories explore how adaptable buildings could respond to changing demand, new uses, disruption and the needs of their occupants.

Each story begins with a practical situation and examines how the separation of structure, accommodation, access and services might create a different response.

About the stories

Illustrations of the system in use

The component definitions describe what ExoSett is. The stories explore what it might allow people, organisations and communities to do.

Some stories describe conventional building uses approached in a different way. Others examine new operational models that become possible when accommodation modules can be installed, removed, replaced or relocated independently.

These stories are illustrative rather than completed building proposals. Their practical implementation would depend on detailed architectural, structural, regulatory, logistical and commercial design.

An evolving collection

Stories will be added as the system develops

The initial stories concentrate on the defining capability of ExoSett: allowing accommodation to change while useful structural, access and service infrastructure remains in place.

Future stories will examine particular building types, operating models, construction processes and relationships between buildings, modules and the organisations that use them.

Each detailed story will identify the situation, the ExoSett response, the components involved, the value created and the practical questions that would need to be resolved.
