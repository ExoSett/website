ExoSett component

Central atrium

A shared roof can turn the space between two or more ExoSett buildings into an enclosed and useful part of a larger complex.

ExoSett buildings may be arranged around a shared central space, with their service frames facing inward and their accommodation frames beyond them.

Principle

Buildings gathered around shared space

The simplest arrangement uses two facing buildings:

Accommodation frame — service frame — atrium — service frame — accommodation frame

The same principle can be extended to any practical number of buildings. Three may form a triangle, four a square or rectangle, five a pentagon, and larger groups may form other polygonal or irregular plans.

A roof spanning the surrounding buildings turns the space between them into an enclosed atrium. The inward-facing service frames provide access, circulation, stairs, lifts, walkways, bridges and entrances around its perimeter.

The atrium may serve as an entrance hall, internal street, winter garden, meeting place, dining area, exhibition space or other shared facility.

This arrangement turns space between ExoSett buildings into valuable shared space within a larger complex. Each accommodation frame remains independently adaptable, while the atrium gives the group a shared architectural and social centre.

Arrangement

An active edge around the atrium

When viewed from above, each side of the atrium would typically follow this order:

Atrium — service frame — accommodation frame

The service frame forms the active edge of the atrium. Its circulation spaces and module entrances may face, overlook and connect with the shared central space.

The enclosure need not always be complete. ExoSett buildings may form only part of the perimeter, leaving one or more sides open or enclosed using a different structure.

Roof and enclosure

A roof shared by the wider complex

The atrium roof spans the combined group of buildings rather than belonging to a single accommodation frame.

Depending on the intended use, the atrium may be fully enclosed, partly enclosed or naturally ventilated. Its roof and end façades may be transparent, translucent or opaque and may be designed as long-lived elements that remain while the surrounding accommodation continues to evolve.

Adaptability

A shared centre with independently adaptable buildings

The central atrium creates a shared architectural whole without removing the independence of the surrounding ExoSett buildings.

Modules may still be installed, removed, refurbished or replaced. Accommodation layouts may change, and individual frames may evolve over time while the atrium continues to provide circulation and shared space for the wider complex.
