Service frame

The service frame provides access, circulation and utility infrastructure for an ExoSett building.

It contains the shared parts of the building needed to reach and operate the accommodation modules, including walkways, stairs, lifts, pipes, cables, ducts and service connections.

How it works

The service frame is positioned alongside one or more accommodation frames. Bridges or thresholds connect it to the entrances of the installed modules, while accessible service routes connect each module to the wider building infrastructure.

Because access and utilities are carried outside the accommodation modules, they can remain available when an individual module is removed or replaced. Work can be carried out at one cell without rebuilding the shared stairs, corridors, risers or service routes used by the rest of the building.

The service frame may be open and simple or enclosed and fully finished, depending on the building and its location. Its form can change, but its purpose remains the same: to provide the continuing operational infrastructure that allows the accommodation to change.

Where the service frame is securely connected to the accommodation frame, it may also contribute to the stiffness and stability of the wider building system. Any such structural contribution would be deliberately engineered.
