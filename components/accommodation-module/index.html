Accommodation module

An accommodation module provides the usable space within an ExoSett building.

It is a removable volumetric unit that can contain a home, hotel room, workspace, classroom, treatment room or another kind of occupied space. Each module is independently supported within a cell of the accommodation frame.

How it works

An accommodation module can be manufactured and substantially fitted out away from the building before being delivered and installed. It may arrive with internal finishes, windows, furniture, sanitary fittings, appliances and building services already in place.

The module connects to the accommodation frame for support and restraint, and to the service frame for access and utilities. These connections are designed so that the module can later be safely disconnected and removed.

A single module may provide a complete room or compact dwelling. Modules in adjacent cells may also be connected to create larger homes, rooms or spaces extending across several floors.

Because the accommodation frame provides the primary structural support, an individual module can be removed, replaced, refurbished or relocated without removing the principal support for the modules around or above it.
