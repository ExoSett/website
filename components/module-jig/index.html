Module jig

A module jig is a temporary installation device used to support, position and guide an accommodation module as it is moved into or out of an ExoSett frame.

The jig holds the module at the required height and orientation while allowing it to travel accurately towards its destination cell.

How it works

Depending upon the installation method, it may run on rails, rollers, bogies or another guided support system. It may also include adjustment mechanisms for levelling, alignment and controlled movement.

The jig helps bring the module’s attachment points into precise alignment with the corresponding frame nodes. This reduces the need for repeated lifting and limits the risk of damage to the module, frame or connection equipment.

During installation, the jig supports the module until its load has been transferred safely to the accommodation frame. During removal, the process is reversed before the module is moved away from the building.

Different module sizes and installation conditions may require different jigs or adjustable configurations. The module jig may operate as part of a larger installation frame or alongside other temporary handling equipment.

Once the installation or removal operation is complete, the module jig is withdrawn and does not form part of the occupied building.
