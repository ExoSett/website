ExoSett installation equipment

Installation frame

The installation frame is temporary handling equipment that docks with an accommodation frame to support the controlled insertion and removal of accommodation modules.

It provides a stable transition between external lifting or transport equipment and the cell in which a module is to be installed or from which it is to be removed.

Definition

A temporary extension of the accommodation frame

An installation frame is a temporary structure or machine used to support and guide an accommodation module during installation or removal.

It docks with defined points on the accommodation frame and aligns its handling surface, rails or other transfer mechanism with the target cell.

Once the operation is complete, the installation frame can be detached and moved to another cell or another building.

Purpose

Make independent module replacement practical

Structural independence is useful only if a module can also be reached, supported, disconnected and moved safely.

The installation frame provides the equipment needed to bridge the gap between the accommodation frame and the crane, vehicle, hoist or other system bringing or removing the module.

Its purpose is to control that transfer, reduce uncontrolled movement and protect the module, accommodation frame, service frame and people involved in the operation.

Key characteristics

What defines an ExoSett installation frame

The installation frame is temporary equipment rather than a permanent part of the occupied building.

It docks with the accommodation frame at defined and structurally capable locations.

It aligns the module with one target cell and supports controlled movement into or out of that cell.

It transfers temporary installation loads without relying on neighbouring accommodation modules.

It can be released after the module has been secured within the accommodation frame.

It may be reusable across several cells, buildings or projects where the relevant interfaces are compatible.

Docking

Establishing a secure relationship with the building

Before a module can be transferred, the installation frame must be positioned accurately and secured to the accommodation frame.

Defined docking points establish the location, level and orientation of the installation equipment relative to the target cell.

The docking connection must resist the vertical, horizontal, torsional and accidental loads that may arise while the module is being handled.

It must also be possible to inspect the connection, confirm that it is fully engaged and release it safely after the operation.

Module transfer

Guiding the module between two support systems

During installation, the module moves from external lifting or transport equipment onto the installation frame and then into the cell.

During removal, the sequence is reversed. The module is released from its permanent supports, transferred onto the installation frame and passed to equipment capable of lowering or transporting it.

Rails, rollers, bogies, skids, sliding surfaces, powered drives, winches or other mechanisms may be used to control this movement.

The transfer system must prevent uncontrolled rolling, sliding, rotation or separation at every stage of the operation.

Installation sequence

A controlled series of temporary conditions

A typical installation begins by preparing the target cell and isolating the working area.

The installation frame is positioned, docked and checked. The module is then brought to the frame, aligned and transferred into the cell.

Once the module reaches its final position, its permanent supports and restraints are engaged. Access and service interfaces can then be completed and tested.

Only after the module is independently secure can the installation frame be released and removed.

The detailed sequence must define responsibility for the module at every point so that it is never left without adequate support or restraint.

Module removal

Designed for departure as well as arrival

Before removal, the module must be taken out of normal operation and prepared for handling.

Occupants and contents are removed as required. Utilities are isolated and disconnected, access connections are separated and any façade or weather-protection elements obstructing the route are opened or removed.

The installation frame is then docked and positioned to receive the module before its permanent restraints and supports are released.

The module can then be withdrawn under control, leaving an empty cell that must be protected until another module is installed or a longer-term closure arrangement is provided.

Temporary loads

Different conditions from normal building use

Module installation creates temporary load cases that may not occur when the building is in ordinary use.

The module may be supported at different locations, partly inside and partly outside the accommodation frame, or subject to movement, impact and eccentric loading.

Wind acting on an exposed module or installation frame may also create significant temporary forces.

The installation frame, docking points and surrounding accommodation frame must therefore be designed for the complete handling sequence, not merely the final installed condition.

Alignment and tolerances

Bringing a large object into a defined cell

A module must pass through the available installation envelope without striking the accommodation frame, service frame, façade, barriers or neighbouring modules.

The installation frame must account for manufacturing tolerances, frame deflection, foundation movement, thermal effects and the accuracy of the external handling equipment.

Guides, adjustable supports and survey references may be used to establish the required position.

Final alignment must allow the module to engage correctly with its permanent supports, restraints, access connection and service interfaces.

Relationship to the service frame

Preserving access while creating an installation route

The service frame must provide access to the module while avoiding permanent obstruction of its installation path.

In some arrangements, the installation frame may approach the accommodation frame from the side opposite the service frame.

In others, access bridges, walkways, barriers, façades or parts of the service frame may need to open, move or be temporarily removed.

The design must maintain safe circulation for the rest of the building or provide an appropriate temporary arrangement while the installation zone is isolated.

External handling

Working with cranes, hoists and transport systems

The installation frame does not necessarily perform the entire lifting or transport operation.

A crane, mast climber, hoist, gantry, mobile platform, vehicle or other system may bring the module to the installation frame and take it away afterwards.

The interfaces between those systems must be defined so that support and control pass safely from one to another.

Site access, lifting radius, wind limits, exclusion zones, ground conditions and transport constraints may all influence the chosen installation method.

Safety

Protecting people and the occupied building

Module installation and removal are planned lifting and construction operations involving large moving loads.

The working area must be isolated from occupants and members of the public. Fall protection, dropped-object controls, temporary guarding, communication and emergency procedures must be provided.

Interlocks, mechanical stops, secondary restraints and monitored connection states may be used to prevent unsafe movement.

The operation must also account for fire safety, weather exposure, service isolation and the temporary effect of an open cell on the occupied building.

Variants

A role that may be performed by different machines

An installation frame may be a relatively simple temporary platform aligned with a low-level cell.

For taller buildings, it may form part of a climbing, suspended or externally supported machine capable of moving between levels.

It may use passive rollers and external pulling equipment or contain its own powered transfer system, controls and adjustable supports.

Some installation frames may be dedicated to one building. Others may be transportable equipment used across a family of compatible ExoSett frames.

Distinction

Not simply a crane or lifting frame

A crane or lifting beam may move a module through the air, but it does not by itself establish the controlled relationship between the module and its target cell.

The installation frame docks with the building, aligns with the cell and provides the final transfer path into or out of the accommodation frame.

It is this building-specific interface that makes the installation frame a distinct part of the ExoSett system.

Development status

A necessary capability requiring detailed machine design

The role of the installation frame within ExoSett is defined, but its detailed engineering remains at an early stage.

Development questions include docking geometry, transfer mechanisms, temporary load paths, alignment, adjustment, controls, external lifting interfaces, weather limits, fall protection and the relationship with façades and service frames.

Different building heights, module masses and site conditions may require substantially different installation-frame designs.

Every practical installation system would require detailed machine, structural, lifting, temporary-works, site-logistics and safety engineering.
