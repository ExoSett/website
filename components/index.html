ExoSett components

The parts of a building designed to change.

ExoSett is made up of distinct structural, accommodation, service and installation components, each with a clearly defined role.

Separating these roles allows buildings to be assembled, adapted, maintained and reconfigured without treating the accommodation itself as a permanent part of the primary structure.

Terminology

Components, roles and interfaces

ExoSett uses precise terminology because similar-looking parts may perform very different roles within the system.

A component is a physical part of an ExoSett building. A role describes what that part does within a particular building. An interface defines how components connect, transfer loads, provide access or exchange services.

A replaceable physical part may occupy a continuing role within the building. For example, one frame node may be removed and replaced, while the frame address and structural role at that location remain unchanged.

Principal elements

The essential ExoSett components

Accommodation frame

The primary load-bearing structure that contains the cells into which accommodation modules are installed.

Accommodation module

A removable volumetric unit that provides a room or other usable space and is independently supported by the accommodation frame.

Service frame

A structure providing access, circulation and routes for services to modules installed in an accommodation frame.

Explore the service frame

Cell

A defined space within an accommodation frame that can host one accommodation module or remain empty.

Explore cells

Frame node

A structural component at an intersection in the frame geometry, connecting columns, beams and other supported components.

Explore frame nodes

Installation frame

Equipment that docks with the accommodation frame and supports the controlled insertion or removal of accommodation modules.

Explore the installation frame

Structure and enclosure

Forming, stabilising and enclosing the building

Column

A vertical structural member that carries loads between frame levels and transfers them towards the foundations.

Beam

A horizontal structural member spanning between frame nodes and helping to define the boundaries of cells.

Inter-node

A frame node positioned between principal frame locations, providing additional structural connections, support points or intermediate frame geometry.

Lashings and bracing

Components that add lateral stiffness and help the frame resist racking, twisting and other horizontal deformation.

The connection through which the accommodation frame transfers loads to its foundations or supporting structure.

Roof

A removable or replaceable covering supported by the uppermost parts of the frame and adapted to the needs of a particular building.

Development

A catalogue that will grow with the system

The components described here define roles within the ExoSett system. Their detailed form, dimensions, materials and connection methods may vary between implementations.

Some components are central to every ExoSett building. Others may be required only by particular applications, structural arrangements or installation methods.

Detailed component pages will record the purpose, characteristics, interfaces, variants and relationships of each part as the system develops.
